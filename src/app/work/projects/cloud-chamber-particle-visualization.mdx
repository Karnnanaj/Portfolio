## Overview

Designed and built a sophisticated cloud chamber apparatus capable of visualizing the tracks of ionizing particles such as cosmic rays and alpha particles. The device uses advanced cooling techniques to maintain optimal conditions for this classic physics demonstration and research tool.

## Key Features

- **Minimum Temperature**: -27°C achieve through cascaded Peltier modules
- **Power Consumption**: 300W operational power
- **Cascaded Peltier Cooling**: Multiple Peltier elements arranged for maximum cooling efficiency
- **Water Cooling Loop**: Integrated water circulation system for heat management
- **Particle Track Visibility**: Optimized optical chamber design for clear track observation


## Operating Principle

The cloud chamber works by creating a supersaturated vapor environment just above the dew point. When ionizing particles pass through the chamber, they ionize air molecules along their path. These ions act as nucleation sites for water vapor condensation, creating visible tracks that reveal the particle's identity, energy, and trajectory.




## Outcome

A fully functional cloud chamber has been successfully constructed that reliably demonstrates particle detection and visualization. The system was able to detect alpha radiation emitted from Americium-241 sample. The achieved temperature of -27°C provides optimal conditions for clear, reproducible particle track visualization.
