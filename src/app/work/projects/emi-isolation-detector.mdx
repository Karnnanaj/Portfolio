## Overview

Developed a comprehensive EMI (Electromagnetic Interference) isolation system for balanced homodyne detectors used in quantum optics experiments. Successfully isolated the detector output from environmental interference, achieving a 13 dB improvement in noise floor performance.

## Problem Statement

Balanced photodetectors are sensitive precision instruments prone to electromagnetic interference from surrounding equipment. The initial setup suffered from noise floor of -55 dBm, severely limiting the detector's sensitivity and measurement capability for quantum optics experiments that require operation at shot-noise levels.

## Key Achievements

- **13 dB Noise Improvement**: Reduced noise floor from -55 dBm to -68 dBm (approaching shot-noise limit)
- **5 GHz Bandwidth Protection**: Complete EMI shielding across operational frequency range
- **Passive Filtering**: Multi-layer approach combining Faraday cages, shielded cables
- **Low Latency Design**: Maintained fast detector response while achieving high noise immunity
- **Reproducible Performance**: Documented procedures for replicating results in similar setups

## Outcome

The EMI isolation retrofit successfully brought the detector performance close to theoretical shot-noise limits, enabling sensitive quantum optical measurements previously impossible with the original setup. The methodology has become standard practice for similar detectors in the laboratory, significantly improving experimental capabilities across multiple research projects.
