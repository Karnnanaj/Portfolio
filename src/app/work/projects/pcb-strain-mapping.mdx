## Overview

Developing an innovative full-field strain mapping methodology for printed circuit boards using Digital Image Correlation (DIC) combined with laser speckle analysis. This technique enables comprehensive characterization of mechanical and thermal stresses across entire PCB surfaces, providing unprecedented insight into component behavior under operating conditions.

## Motivation

Traditional strain measurement techniques (strain gauges, FEA simulation) provide only point-wise data or theoretical predictions. Full-field strain mapping captures the complete spatial stress distribution, enabling:
- Identification of stress concentration zones
- Validation of finite element models
- Optimization of PCB layout and component placement
- Early detection of potential failure points
- Real-time thermal stress characterization



## Outcome

Successfully developed a novel full-field strain mapping capability for PCB characterization. The technique provides unprecedented insight into thermal and mechanical stress distribution, enabling data-driven PCB design optimization and enhanced reliability. This methodology is now being applied to validate designs before manufacturing, reducing field failures and improving product robustness.
