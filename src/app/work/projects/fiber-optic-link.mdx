## Overview

Designed and implemented a rugged fiber-optic communication system for remote vehicle operation, leveraging Small Form-factor Pluggable (SFP) transceiver technology. The system provides extended range, high-bandwidth data transmission with immunity to electromagnetic interference, enabling reliable real-time telemetry and control for vehicles operating at distances up to 20 kilometers.


## Key Achievements

- **20 km Transmission Range**: Extended communication distance suitable for remote vehicle operations in open-field and industrial environments
- **1 GHz Bandwidth Capacity**: High-speed data channel supporting simultaneous video, telemetry, and command streams
- **Hot-Swappable Architecture**: SFP transceiver design enables rapid module replacement without system shutdown
- **EMI Immunity**: Fiber-optic isolation eliminates electromagnetic interference coupling to vehicle electronics
- **Ruggedized Design**: Weather-sealed connectors and redundant transmission paths for field reliability
- **Plug-and-Play Configuration**: Standardized SFP form factor simplifies integration with existing vehicle control systems

## Outcome

The fiber-optic link successfully extended vehicle operational range from ~5 km (copper baseline) to 20 km while dramatically improving data integrity and eliminating RF interference issues. The hot-swappable SFP design reduced field maintenance time and enabled rapid deployment across diverse remote vehicle platforms. This solution has become the standard communication backbone for Bravecore's extended-range vehicle operations, supporting both commercial and research applications.
